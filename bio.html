Childhood Years

Hello, I’m Sean Simoun Lewis N. Doctor1 My childhood wasn’t very exciting, and I don’t remember much of it because it wasn’t really fun.2 However, I still enjoyed my early years despite the challenges.3 My mother and father met in Baguio, and they decided to live together quickly because they loved each other.4 I have five siblings, and my youngest sibling is 10 years younger than me.5 There were many things I wanted to try, but because we didn’t have enough money, I couldn’t do them.5 I always wanted to play in a mall park with slides and small balls.6 When I was five years old, both of my grandparents on my father’s side passed away7 Even though I don’t remember them well, I still wish I could have shown them my childhood life8 I wanted them to see my grades, my friends, and my experiences.9 I never felt my grandparents’ love because they lived far from our hometown.10 I also wanted to learn how to ride a bike, but I never got the chance.11 My father was always busy working to provide for our family.12 Because of this, he had no time to teach me how to ride a bike or do fun activities.13 I did not go to a public school because my mother was worried about my future.14 At that time, public schools were not considered the best option.15 When I was in Grade 5, I dreamed of becoming a chef.16 We had a school project where we had to cook a meal for our teachers.18 I got a perfect score, and that made me happy.19 That moment made me realize that I loved cooking and wanted to be a chef.20

Teenage Years

When I turned 13 and entered Grade 7, my interests started to change.1 I realized that studying was harder than I expected2 I often struggled to focus and understand my lessons, and I didn’t know why.3 Even now, I still find studying difficult4 In Grade 8, I had one of the best times in school because I enjoyed table tennis very much.5 It was my main sport, and I even made it to the finals in our DAOP tournament—and I won.6 However, in Grade 9, I experienced something terrifying that left me traumatized.7 One of my classmates was possessed, and it scared me a lot.8 She and her friends went to the restroom, and they saw the door lock moving on its own.9 There was no airflow in the hallway, yet the school staff said it was just the wind.10 However, I saw it clearly with my own two eyes—or rather, four eyes since I was already wearing glasses.11 That experience made me believe in ghosts for the first time.12 On March 5, 2020, the pandemic started, and that was the beginning of my boring teenage life13 We had online classes, and it was the most boring time of my life. Every day, I had to wake up, face my device, hurt my eyes, and hurt my back from sitting too long.14 My table tennis career ended because I didn’t know how to continue after the pandemic.15 By the time the pandemic was almost over, I was in Grade 12, second semester.16 Finally, we had face-to-face classes at my school, University of the East – Manila.17 I enjoyed it, but not that much because I didn’t have many friends back then.18 I also didn’t know where I belonged.19 Some of my Junior High School classmates were also my schoolmates, but I was a late enrollee because I took time choosing where to study. 20That’s why I didn’t have close friends to rely on, especially when it came to academics.21

College Years

College years are the best because you will meet a lot of friends, a lot of enemies, and competitive people.1 But at the end of the day, you will still learn things the hard way.2 College has taught me many lessons, and one of them is that learning is never easy.3 The program I chose is very challenging, and I am still struggling with how to study properly.4 I have tried many different ways to improve my study habits, both hard and easy methods.5 I even stayed awake for 24 hours just to see if I could remember my lectures better.6 College has also taught me how to do things I never did before, like drinking alcohol.7 University life is like living in a barangay—there are so many Marites spreading gossip.8 When a rumor starts, it spreads to all students in just an hour.9 College is fun, but it can also be hard, especially when you have a childhood friend.10 Our busy schedules make it difficult to see each other. 11This is tough for me because my childhood friends were always by my side growing up12 Now, when I need them, I can’t see them because they are busy or spending time with new friends.13 I always think about when or how I will graduate because, right now, I am still unsure14 After college, I plan to pursue my dreams in the UAE with my sister.15 My goal is to gain job experience here first before leaving the country.16 I visited the UAE last year, and I saw that life there is good.17 I am ready to leave my loved ones behind to make my life easier.18 Even though it will be hard to say goodbye, I believe it is the best decision for my future.19
